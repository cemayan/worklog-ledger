Privacy Policy

Worklog Ledger for Jira — last updated 2026-07-19

Worklog Ledger is built so that your data never leaves your browser except to talk to services you already use. There is no Worklog Ledger server.

What the extension accesses

  • Jira Cloud (*.atlassian.net): the extension calls Jira's REST API from your browser to read and write worklogs, using your existing Jira session or an API token you enter. Requests go directly from your browser to Atlassian — never through any intermediary.

What the extension stores

  • Locally in your browser (chrome.storage): your Jira site URL, optional API token, display preferences, client/rate mappings, and worklogs you have staged but not yet pushed. Synced storage may replicate settings across your own Chrome profile via your Google account, as with any extension.

What we collect

Nothing. The extension contains no analytics, no tracking, and no error reporting that sends data anywhere.

Payments & license check

Paid plans are sold by Paddle as merchant of record; your payment details are processed by Paddle under its own privacy policy. To unlock paid features, the extension sends only the email address you used at checkout to our license endpoint (a Cloudflare Worker) and receives back a yes/no subscription status. No Jira data, tokens or usage information is ever included in that request.

Changes & contact

Material changes to this policy will be noted in the extension's changelog. Questions: see the support link on the Chrome Web Store listing.
